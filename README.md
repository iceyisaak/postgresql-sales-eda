# PostgreSQL Sales EDA

###### Dataset: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/sahirmaharajj/retail-sales-analysis/data

###### Repo: https://github.com/iceyisaak/postgresql-sales-eda
---

### Tech Stack
- **Tools**: pgAdmin4
- **Database**: PostgreSQL
- **Language**: SQL (CTEs, Window Functions, Aggregations)
- **Concept**: Explorative Data Analysis (EDA)

---

### Objectives
- A PostgreSQL-driven EDA project focused on retail sales optimization. By querying and visualizing sales trends and customer demographics, I identified significant growth opportunities and provided a strategic framework for data-driven decision-making in a competitive retail environment.

---

### Questions Addressed:
1. What is total Number of Sales?
2. How many unique customers are there?
3. How many categories are there?
4. Which sales are made on '2022-11-05'?
5. Which transactions where category is 'Clothing' and the quantity are more than 4 in NOV 2022?
6. Show the total sales for each category
7. What is the average age of customers who purchased items from the 'Beauty' category?
8. Which transaction where total sale is more than 1000?
9. How many transactions are there in each gender and each category?
10. What is the average sale for each month?
11. Show the Top 5 Customers by highest total_sales
12. Show the no. of unique customers who bought items from each category
13. Show the no. of orders between different shifts of the day

---
### CONCLUSION & RECOMMENDATION

More insights can be obtained by exploring the sales data. Each year can reveal different insights. Here are the general overview of the sales in 2022 and 2023:

1. Sales can vary throughout different years. In 2022, July had the most sales. But in 2023, February had the most sales of the year.
2. On a given day, most sales are usually made in the evening, after 17:00.
3. The top 3 product categories by sales are: Clothing, Electronics, and Beauty
